The Birth of Cryptologicus

It all started when my nephew Sam, studying computer science at university, asked me a technical question about a web protocol. When I gave him an answer, he immediately challenged it—not because he knew better, but because a guy on YouTube disagreed with me.

"Why do you think that guy is right and I'm not?" I asked. "Is it just because he made a video?" By the expression on his face, I could tell that's exactly why he trusted the YouTuber—and that he realized how flawed that reasoning was.

"Well, that guy is no different from me," I told him. "In fact, he likely knows less than I do. I've probably been doing this stuff longer, my experience is more varied, and I know this like the back of my hand." Sam's retort was perfect: "Well, he's got a website and you haven't."

Touché. My immediate reaction was: "Well, not for much longer, he hasn't." And that's how Cryptologicus was born.
