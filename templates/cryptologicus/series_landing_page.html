Prime Predicament: Cracking a SANS RSA CTF from First Principles

A multi-part walkthrough of the Prime Predicament CTF from SANS Cyberthreat 2024 – blending story, maths, and real command-line workflows to show how a “hopeless” encrypted file becomes a solvable puzzle.

Cryptography challenges have a particular appeal: they’re equal parts logic puzzle, mathematical hunt, and detective story. Prime Predicament, the SANS Cyberthreat 2024 CTF challenge, is a perfect example — a deceptively simple starting point (“Here’s an encrypted file. Here’s a public key. Off you go.”) that unravels into a sequence of discoveries about RSA, prime arithmetic, tooling, and the delicate assumptions that underpin modern cryptography. What begins as a puzzle quickly becomes a guided tour through both the elegance and fragility of real-world crypto systems.

This series walks through the challenge step-by-step, showing not only how the encryption was broken, but why each choice, command, or hypothesis mattered. You’ll see how a disciplined methodology — inspection, hypothesis, validation, refinement — turns a seemingly impossible task into a structured path forward. Whether you’re new to cryptography or already comfortable with key structures and number theory, the series is written to be both approachable and technically rigorous.

Across these instalments, we’ll explore PEM structures, modulus properties, key misuse, entropy considerations, and practical exploitation techniques — all tied together with real command-line workflows, tools, and diagrams. Prime Predicament isn’t just a write-up; it’s a learning journey designed to sharpen your instinct for cryptographic problem-solving and illuminate the fascinating mechanics behind RSA and modern encryption.
